## Key contributions

- Observes that naively-added speculative decoding can degrade serving performance because it adds overhead and speculated tokens may miss
- Introduces a closed-loop speculation control system that profiles the execution environment and adjusts speculation amount at runtime
- Predicts 'goodput' (successfully generated tokens) to dynamically tune the amount of intra-request parallelism at runtime, implemented on vLLM with consistent improvements across diverse workloads and hardware
