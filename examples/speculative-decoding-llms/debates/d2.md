Does speculative decoding only help at small batch sizes, or can it accelerate high-throughput, large-batch serving?

### Speculation helps mainly at small batch sizes and is fragile under high throughput
Conventional wisdom and serving-systems analysis hold that intra-request speculation overhead competes with batching parallelism and can degrade serving performance if added naively without tuning.

*Held by:* [[papers/liu2024]], [[papers/sadhukhan2024]]

### Speculation can accelerate even high-throughput regimes for moderate-to-long sequences
MagicDec shows that by addressing the KV bottleneck (which scales with both batch size and sequence length) with a sparse-KV draft model, speculative decoding achieves up to 2.51x speedup at batch sizes from 32 to 256, and EAGLE-3 reports throughput gains at batch size 64.

*Held by:* [[papers/sadhukhan2024]], [[papers/li2025]]
