Diffusion language models still lag autoregressive models in exact likelihood / perplexity modeling, and proper likelihood evaluation is itself unresolved: the ELBO is a loose bound computed under the training rather than test-time distribution, so reported perplexities can be misleading.

**Why it matters:** Likelihood is the canonical training and evaluation signal for LMs; without a proper, comparable likelihood, claims that diffusion LMs match AR quality remain contestable and cross-family comparison is unreliable.
