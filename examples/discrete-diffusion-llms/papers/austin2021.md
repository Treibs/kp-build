## Key contributions

- Introduces D3PMs, diffusion-like generative models for discrete data that generalize multinomial diffusion by allowing structured (non-uniform) corruption transition matrices
- Defines absorbing-state (masking) corruption among the transition-matrix families, the basis of later masked diffusion LMs
- Draws a formal connection between discrete diffusion models and autoregressive / mask-based generative models
- Shows the choice of transition matrix is a key design decision improving results in image and text domains
