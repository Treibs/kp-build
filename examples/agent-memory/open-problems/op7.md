Memory evaluation itself is immature: prior benchmarks are limited in memory levels, interactive scenarios, and metric diversity, and most measure single axes rather than effectiveness, efficiency, and capacity jointly.

**Why it matters:** Without benchmarks that test participation vs. observation, factual vs. reflective memory, and cost/capacity alongside accuracy, reported gains may not transfer to real long-horizon use. The proliferation of new benchmarks (LoCoMo, LongMemEval, MemBench, MemoryAgentBench) signals the community has not converged on how to measure memory.
