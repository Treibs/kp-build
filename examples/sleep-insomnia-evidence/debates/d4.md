Should insomnia be treated first with behavioral therapy or with medication — and where do newer orexin antagonists fit?

### Behavioral therapy (CBT-I) is first-line; drugs are adjunctive
ACP strongly recommends CBT-I as the initial treatment with pharmacotherapy added only via shared decision-making if CBT-I fails, and the AASM frames all drug recommendations as merely WEAK with downgraded evidence — establishing CBT-I's primacy.

*Held by:* [[papers/qaseem2016]], [[papers/sateia2017]]

### Newer dual orexin antagonists are effective drug options with favorable safety, but do not displace first-line behavioral therapy
Daridorexant 25-50 mg and lemborexant improved objective and subjective sleep with comparable-to-placebo adverse events and sustained efficacy, and the network meta-analysis ranks lemborexant favorably — yet the same evidence notes drugs are used more because of inadequate resources, not because they are preferred first-line.

*Held by:* [[papers/mignot2022]], [[papers/karppa2020]], [[papers/decrescenzo2022]]
