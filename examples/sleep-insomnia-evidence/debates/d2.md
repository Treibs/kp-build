Do blue-light-blocking glasses actually improve sleep, or only shift a biomarker / subjective perception?

### No significant objective sleep benefit
A 2025 meta-analysis of crossover RCTs found only a non-significant reduction in sleep onset latency and concluded current RCT evidence does not support significant effects; an individual healthy-adult RCT likewise found no improvement in objective sleep time or quality.

*Held by:* [[papers/lunarangel2025]], [[papers/bigalke2021]]

### There is a real mechanistic/subjective signal
Blocking evening blue light produced an earlier onset and rise of melatonin in pregnant women (a genuine circadian effect), and one RCT found a small reduction in self-reported sleep onset — suggesting the mechanism is real even if downstream objective sleep gains are inconsistent.

*Held by:* [[papers/liset2022]], [[papers/bigalke2021]]
