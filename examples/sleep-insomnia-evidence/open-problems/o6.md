The evidence for popular consumer interventions like mouth-taping and blue-light-blocking glasses is thin, underpowered, or null, and adequately powered standardized RCTs are lacking.

**Why it matters:** Mouth-taping rests on only a handful of small studies with a potentially serious harm signal, and blue-light-blocking glasses show non-significant actigraphic effects in pooled crossover trials; the gap between social-media-driven adoption and rigorous evidence leaves clinicians unable to advise confidently.
