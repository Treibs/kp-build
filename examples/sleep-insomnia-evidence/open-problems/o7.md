Tracker-driven preoccupation with sleep ('orthosomnia') is an emerging clinical problem that lacks established prevalence data and validated management pathways.

**Why it matters:** Patients increasingly trust device data over validated measurement and develop perfectionistic sleep anxiety; a measurement scale (BOS) now exists, but how to prevent and treat orthosomnia — and whether trackers can be safely folded into CBT-I — remains underdeveloped.
