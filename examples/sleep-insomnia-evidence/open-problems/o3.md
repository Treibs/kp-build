Consumer wearable sleep trackers cannot reliably substitute for polysomnography, particularly for detecting wake and characterizing sleep stages.

**Why it matters:** Across home and lab validations, devices show high sleep sensitivity but low wake specificity and systematically overestimate total sleep time/efficiency; without solving wake detection and stage classification they cannot serve as stand-alone diagnostic tools, and substitution for actigraphy in insomnia is still unproven.
