The quality and consistency of evidence (and of the products themselves) for supplements is too poor to support firm recommendations.

**Why it matters:** The magnesium evidence base is rated low to very-low quality with high risk of bias, and melatonin supplements vary wildly from their labels (and can contain serotonin), so even a 'modest effect' cannot be reliably delivered by what consumers actually buy.
