## Key contributions

- Meta-analysis of 19 randomized placebo-controlled trials (1683 subjects) quantifying melatonin's hypnotic effect on primary sleep disorders
- Found melatonin produces statistically significant but small reductions in sleep latency (WMD ~7 min) and increases in total sleep time (WMD ~8 min)
- Established the 'modest effect' framing: melatonin's absolute benefit is smaller than other pharmacological insomnia treatments but with a more benign side-effect profile
- Meta-regression found longer-duration and higher-dose trials showed greater effects on latency and total sleep time, but not on sleep quality
