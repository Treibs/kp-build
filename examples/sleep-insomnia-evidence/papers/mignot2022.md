## Key contributions

- Two pivotal phase 3 RCTs (n=930 and n=924) establishing daridorexant, a dual orexin receptor antagonist, for insomnia disorder at the dose later approved
- Daridorexant 50 mg significantly reduced polysomnographic wake time after sleep onset (WASO) and latency to persistent sleep, and improved self-reported total sleep time and daytime sleepiness (IDSIQ), versus placebo at months 1 and 3
- The 10 mg dose showed no significant effect on the primary PSG endpoints, demonstrating a dose-response and the importance of the 25-50 mg range
- Adverse-event incidence was comparable to placebo with nasopharyngitis and headache most common, supporting a favorable safety profile distinct from benzodiazepines/Z-drugs
