## Key contributions

- Coins the term 'orthosomnia' and describes a clinical case series of patients seeking treatment for self-diagnosed sleep disturbances based on consumer sleep-tracker data
- Documents that patients often trust tracker data over validated techniques such as polysomnography or actigraphy, and may develop a perfectionistic quest for ideal sleep
- Frames the clinical challenge of educating patients about device validity while managing their enthusiasm for objective data, and recommends incorporating trackers into CBT-I
