## Key contributions

- Sleep Research Society commissioned State of the Science review synthesizing what wearable sleep-tracking data can provide, how accurate it is, and how to incorporate it into research — the authoritative measurement-methodology reference
- States consumer-grade devices can exceed traditional actigraphy in assessing PSG-defined sleep, while cataloging clear limitations: misclassification of wakefulness, problems tracking outside the main sleep bout, artifacts, and unclear generalization across populations
- Flags an equity/individual-variability concern: person-specific factors such as skin color or obesity can degrade sensor performance and potentially augment existing healthcare disparities
