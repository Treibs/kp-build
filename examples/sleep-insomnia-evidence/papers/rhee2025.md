## Key contributions

- PRISMA systematic review of nighttime mouth taping for mouth breathing, sleep-disordered breathing, and OSA; 10 studies (213 patients) met inclusion criteria after screening 120 articles
- Found only 2 studies showing statistically significant improvement in sleep apnea markers (AHI, oxygen desaturations), while other studies showed no difference
- Identified a potentially serious risk of harm, including asphyxiation in the presence of nasal obstruction, and notes many studies excluded patients with nasal obstruction
- Concludes evidence is insufficient and calls the social-media-driven practice potentially unsafe for indiscriminate use
