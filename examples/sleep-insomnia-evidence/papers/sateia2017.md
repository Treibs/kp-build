## Key contributions

- GRADE-based AASM guideline giving drug-specific (not class-level) recommendations for chronic insomnia pharmacotherapy
- All pharmacologic recommendations are WEAK, reflecting downgraded evidence quality due to industry funding/publication-bias risk, few trials per agent, and heterogeneity
- Suggests suvorexant (orexin antagonist) for sleep-maintenance insomnia and specific Z-drugs/benzodiazepines for onset/maintenance, while recommending against melatonin, diphenhydramine, valerian, tryptophan, trazodone, and tiagabine
- Frames drug therapy as adjunctive/conditional, establishing context for behavioral therapy as the preferred first-line approach
