## Key contributions

- Validates a consumer tracker (Fitbit Alta HR) and research actigraph (Actiwatch Spectrum Pro) against polysomnography in 42 adults with insomnia disorder — a population where validation was previously lacking
- Documents the core actigraphy/wearable measurement problem in insomnia: high sleep-detection sensitivity but low wake-detection specificity (39-45%), with both devices overestimating total sleep time and sleep efficiency and underestimating wake after sleep onset
- Finds Fitbit sleep-stage classification underestimates deep/REM and overestimates light sleep, and states substitution for actigraphy in insomnia requires further investigation
