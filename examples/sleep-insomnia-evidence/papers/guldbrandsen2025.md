## Key contributions

- Develops and validates the Bergen Orthosomnia Scale (BOS), the first psychometric instrument for measuring orthosomnia (excessive preoccupation with sleep), across three studies (Delphi panel, 994 respondents, 473-person validation)
- Identifies a two-factor structure ('interference' and 'rigidity'), each six items, with acceptable model fit, good internal consistency, and 3-week test-retest reliability
- Establishes construct validity: orthosomnia factors correlate positively with sleep effort, dysfunctional beliefs about sleep, perfectionism, OCD, and health anxiety, with the interference factor also linked to insomnia and neuroticism
