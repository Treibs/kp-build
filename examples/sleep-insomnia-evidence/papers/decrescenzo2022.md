## Key contributions

- Largest network meta-analysis of insomnia pharmacotherapy: 154 double-blind RCTs, 30 interventions, 44,089 participants, with certainty graded via the CINeMA framework
- For acute treatment, benzodiazepines, eszopiclone, lemborexant, seltorexant, zolpidem, zopiclone (and others) beat placebo on sleep quality (SMD range 0.36-0.83); benzodiazepines/Z-drugs outperformed melatonin, ramelteon, and zaleplon
- Found eszopiclone and lemborexant had the most favorable acute/long-term profile, but flagged eszopiclone's adverse-event burden, inconclusive long-term lemborexant safety, and poor tolerability or absent long-term data for benzodiazepines, daridorexant, and suvorexant
- Concluded melatonin, ramelteon, and non-licensed drugs showed no overall material benefit, providing an evidence base to rank drug options
