## Key contributions

- Tests four consumer wearables (Fatigue Science Readiband, Fitbit Inspire HR, Oura ring, Polar Vantage V Titan) plus research-grade actigraphy against a mobile EEG headband under naturalistic at-home conditions, addressing the lab-vs-real-world measurement gap
- Quantifies the wake-detection weakness: high epoch-by-epoch sleep sensitivity (0.93-0.96) but low wake specificity (0.35-0.45) across all devices
- Concludes high variability in sleep-stage tracking means devices are best used for sleep-wake outcomes, not sleep stages — a concrete limit on consumer-device measurement claims
