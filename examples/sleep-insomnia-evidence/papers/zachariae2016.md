## Key contributions

- Meta-analysis of 11 RCTs (1460 participants) evaluating internet-delivered CBT-I (eCBT-I), framing digital delivery explicitly as a strategy to overcome the access/scalability barrier of CBT-I
- Reports eCBT-I effect sizes (Hedges g) ranging from 0.21 to 1.09 across sleep outcomes, comparable to face-to-face CBT-I and generally maintained at 4-48 week follow-up
- Identifies moderators: longer treatment duration and higher degree of personal clinical support associated with larger effects, while larger intervention-group dropout predicts smaller effects — a direct engagement/scalability tension
