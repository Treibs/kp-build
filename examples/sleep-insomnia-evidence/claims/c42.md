In adults with insomnia disorder, both a consumer tracker and a research actigraph showed high sleep-detection sensitivity but low wake-detection specificity against polysomnography, overestimating total sleep time and sleep efficiency.

> Compared with polysomnography, both activity trackers displayed high accuracy (81.12% versus 82.80%, AWS and FBA respectively; ns) and sensitivity (sleep detection; 96.66% versus 96.04%, respectively; ns) but low specificity (wake detection; 39.09% versus 44.76%, respectively; p = .037). Both trackers overestimated total sleep time and sleep efficiency, and underestimated sleep latency and wake after sleep onset.
