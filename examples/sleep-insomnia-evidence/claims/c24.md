The review concluded that licensed drugs including benzodiazepines, daridorexant, and suvorexant can be effective for acute insomnia but are associated with poor tolerability or lack long-term safety data.

> Many licensed drugs (including benzodiazepines, daridorexant, suvorexant, and trazodone) can be effective in the acute treatment of insomnia but are associated with poor tolerability, or information about long-term effects is not available.

— [[papers/decrescenzo2022]] (doi:10.1016/s0140-6736(22)00878-9)
