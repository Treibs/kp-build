## The plain-terms story

Ask an AI for a Sui contract today and it may well write last year's Sui. The counter-contract proof: an unaided model writing a plain counter contract from parametric memory produced pre-2024-edition Move — `struct Counter` — which fails to compile on sui mainnet-v1.74.1 (visibility annotations are mandatory in the 2024 edition). One mechanical fix (`public struct`) and the same contract builds green. The pack is a receipt-backed cheat sheet: every rule already ran through the real compiler, and every rule ships with the broken form the model would otherwise write.

## The RED/GREEN two-sided gate

Each rule carries a GREEN fixture (must compile); 28 of 35 rules also carry a RED fixture (must FAIL with a pinned error fragment in `expected_error.txt`). The refresh mechanic: Sui ships a new mainnet toolchain every ~2 weeks (the full re-verify loop is specified in [CONTEXT.md](CONTEXT.md)). This is the first pack whose staleness check is mechanical in both directions — with one disclosed exception: the test-scenario beat's `#[test]` body is not compiled by the plain-build gate (see [claims/test-scenario-green.md](claims/test-scenario-green.md)).

**Revision beat (2026-07-07).** That gap is now closed by the fixture-proven `receiving` beat, added *after* the falsification and clearly separated from it (the numbers above were measured on the 44-claim pack): `public_receive(&mut parent.id, ticket)` with `ticket: transfer::Receiving<T>` and `T: key + store` — RED: cross-module `transfer::receive` fails with `error[Sui E02009]: invalid private transfer call`, the exact error both falsification arms hit. Provenance in [`examples/sui-move-fixtures/beat-log.md`](../sui-move-fixtures/beat-log.md).

**Deepening round 1 (2026-07-07).** A structured residual-gap probe (15 fresh tasks across five unprobed territories, dual model, pack loaded, pinned oracle — protocol and ledger in `docs/deepening/round-1/`) surfaced 8 compile failures for claude-haiku-4-5 that collapsed to 5 root-cause families, of which 4 were beat-worthy (the 5th was a loaded rule ignored, recorded not taught); a 5th beat came from the warning-tier count. Each beat is fixture-proven against the pinned compiler before commit: `use-self` (Move group-import `Self` keyword), `type-name` (`TypeName` vs `String` + the 1.74.1 `get` → `with_defining_ids` deprecation), `witness-naming` (plain witnesses must not collide with the OTW naming convention, Sui E02005), `key-field-store` (every field of a `key` struct needs `store`, E05001), and `dynamic-field-exists` (grounding-only: the 1.74.1 `exists_` → `exists` rename, warning-tier). Pack grew 47 → 61 claims. Same honesty invariant as the revision beat: experiment 1/2 numbers were measured on the pre-deepening pack. The deepening's held-out effect was then measured by pre-registered experiment 3 (table above): deepened pack 4/6 vs pre-deepening pack 2/6 compile-pass for claude-haiku-4-5, with the taught `use-self` corner recurring only in the pre-deepening arm.

**Deepening round 2 (2026-07-07).** A second gap-seeded probe (15 fresh tasks across five territories chosen from the recorded experiment-3 falsification gaps, dual model, pack loaded, pinned oracle — protocol and ledger in `docs/deepening/round-2/`) surfaced 8 compile failures for claude-haiku-4-5 (sonnet 15/15) yielding 9 fixture-proven beats: seven compile-tier — `module-address-form` (E02004 bare module declaration), `option-field-fill` (E05001 Option-field overwrite needs `drop`; fix is `fill`), `table-key-by-value` (E04007 `sui::table` keys pass by value), `string-module-path` (E03006 `String::utf8` vs `string::utf8`), `string-append` (E04003 `+` is integer-only; String concatenation is `append`), `block-statement-semicolon` (E01002 braced statements need `;` mid-sequence), `public-transfer-foreign` (Sui E02009 foreign `store` types need `public_transfer` — the mirror of the `ownership-transfer` rule) — and two warning-tier grounding-only beats, `transfer-composability` (Lint W99001) and `vector-literal` (W04037 `vector::empty` deprecation). Two exp-3 territories did NOT reproduce and are recorded clean: the E04024 `let mut` borrow shape (0 hits in 6 runs) and W99003 Balance-vs-Coin. Pack grew 61 → 86 claims. Same honesty invariant: the experiment-3 numbers above were measured on the 61-claim pack. Round 2's held-out effect was then measured by pre-registered experiment 4 (table above): **no headline** — kp86 tied kp61 on both metrics (3/6 primary, 2/6 secondary) and the base arm beat both pack arms (5/6). The failure composition still moved the way round 2 intended (the taught `option-field-fill` class fired only in the pre-round-2 arm — 3 hits across 2 tasks, both of which passed under the 86-claim pack — and no taught error class from any round recurred in the kp86 arm), but the pack arms lost those gains on new import-path and API-existence classes — several plausibly induced by the pack's own idiom vocabulary (recorded as an observation, not a measured mechanism, in the experiment README).

**Deepening round 3 (2026-07-08).** A third gap-seeded probe (15 fresh tasks across five territories chosen from the recorded experiment-4 candidates plus two carried promotion-rule deferrals, dual model, pack loaded, pinned oracle — protocol and ledger in `docs/deepening/round-3/`) surfaced 5 valid compile failures for claude-haiku-4-5 and 1 for claude-sonnet-4-6 (one task, import-3, was VOID for both models: the task itself pinned `module match::donation` and `match` is a reserved keyword — a task-authoring defect disclosed in the triage, not a model failure). Four fixture-proven beats: `missing-module-import` (E03006 — a `module::fn` alias call needs `use sui::module`; the omission sibling of round 1's `use-self`), `destructure-ignore` (E05001 — `field: _` cannot ignore a non-`drop` value; `UID` needs `object::delete`), `implicit-field-copy` (E05001 — dot-operator reads copy, so non-`copy` fields move out by destructuring; promoted on recurrence from experiment 4), and `param-mut` (E04024 — Move 2024's `mut` requirement covers by-value function parameters; promoted on recurrence from the experiment-3 record). Two territories were recorded clean in the probe with the pack loaded (`generic-transfer-key-bound`, `clock-epoch-confusion`) — though the tier-1 remeasure then saw `generic-transfer-key-bound` fire once (×3 cumulative; top round-4 candidate); one round-2 taught class recurred in the probe and was recorded, not re-taught (`block-statement-semicolon`), and the tier-1 remeasure recorded one round-1 taught class recurring under the loaded pack (`use-self`). Pack grew 86 → 98 claims. Same honesty invariant: the experiment-4 numbers above were measured on the 86-claim pack. Round 3's held-out effect was then measured by pre-registered experiment 5 (table above): **no headline** — kp98 tied kp86 on compile-pass (3/6 = 3/6) and regressed on clean-compile (1/6 vs 3/6; all three lost-cleanliness warnings are `Lint W99001`, the class the loaded round-2 `transfer-composability` beat teaches). Both pack arms beat base on the primary (3/6 vs 2/6), with the flipped task failing in base on the original `struct-visibility` class. Zero error-tier taught classes recurred in the kp98 arm, but the kp86 arm recorded three loaded-rule-ignored classes (`use-self`, `option-field-fill`, `block-statement-semicolon`), and the pre-registered vocabulary-induction analysis fit 0 of 6 failing pack-arm rows (experiment 4's post-hoc 3-of-6 fit did not replicate). After two consecutive no-headline experiments, the recorded forks for the next unit of work are a small targeted round 4 (SUI-import + Rust-bleed + hash corner) or a payload-salience/slimming experiment on the existing 98 claims — detail in the experiment README.
