Incretin-based pharmacotherapy has transformed obesity treatment over the past decade. Glucagon-like peptide-1 (GLP-1) receptor agonists, originally developed for type 2 diabetes, reduce body weight through central appetite suppression, delayed gastric emptying, and enhanced satiety signaling via hypothalamic and hindbrain circuits. Subcutaneous semaglutide 2.4 mg weekly was the first agent to demonstrate transformative efficacy: the STEP-1 trial (10.1056/NEJMoa2032183, Wilding et al., 2021) reported mean weight loss of ~14.9% over 68 weeks versus 2.4% with placebo. Glucose-dependent insulinotropic polypeptide (GIP) co-agonism amplifies these effects. Tirzepatide, a dual GIP/GLP-1 receptor agonist, achieved up to ~20.9% weight loss at the 15 mg dose in SURMOUNT-1 (10.1056/NEJMoa2206038, Jastreboff et al., 2022), establishing dual agonism as superior to GLP-1 monotherapy. This was confirmed head-to-head in SURMOUNT-5 (Aronne et al., 2025), where tirzepatide produced ~20.2% versus ~13.7% weight loss with semaglutide.

Cardiovascular benefit independent of diabetes was demonstrated in the landmark SELECT trial (10.1056/NEJMoa2307563, Lincoff et al., 2023), which enrolled patients with established cardiovascular disease and overweight/obesity but without diabetes; semaglutide 2.4 mg reduced major adverse cardiovascular events by 20%. Additional cardiometabolic indications followed, including heart failure with preserved ejection fraction (STEP-HFpEF, 10.1056/NEJMoa2306963, Kosiborod et al., 2023) and the SOUL trial of oral semaglutide for cardiovascular outcomes (2025). Tirzepatide showed benefit in obesity-related HFpEF in SUMMIT (10.1056/NEJMoa2410027, Packer et al., 2024) and improved sleep apnea in SURMOUNT-OSA (10.1056/NEJMoa2404881, Malhotra et al., 2024).

Adverse effects are predominantly gastrointestinal—nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, constipation—and typically dose-dependent and transient. A central limitation is weight regain after discontinuation: the STEP-1 extension (Wilding et al., 2022) and STEP-4 (10.1001/jama.2021.3224, Rubino et al., 2021) showed that stopping treatment leads to substantial regain, reframing obesity as a chronic disease requiring sustained therapy.

Emerging agents target convenience and greater efficacy. Oral semaglutide 50 mg (OASIS-1, Knop et al., 2023) and the small-molecule orforglipron showed meaningful oral weight loss. The triple GIP/GLP-1/glucagon agonist retatrutide achieved ~24% weight loss in phase 2 (10.1056/NEJMoa2301972, Jastreboff et al., 2023), and CagriSema (semaglutide plus the amylin analog cagrilintide) advanced in the REDEFINE program, suggesting a continuing escalation in achievable weight loss.
