Incretin-based pharmacotherapy has transformed obesity treatment by exploiting the physiology of gut-derived incretin hormones. Glucagon-like peptide-1 (GLP-1) receptor agonists augment glucose-dependent insulin release while reducing glucagon secretion and gastric emptying, and—critically for obesity—they inhibit food intake and reduce body weight, actions that are highly conserved across obese animals and humans (drucker2022, 10.1016/j.molmet.2021.101351). These effects act through both direct tissue signaling and indirect neuronal and endocrine pathways (campbell2013, 10.1016/j.cmet.2013.04.008). Dual agonism extends this mechanism: the GIP/GLP-1 co-agonist tirzepatide (LY3298176) activates both receptors and decreased body weight more potently than a GLP-1 agonist in mice (coskun2018, 10.1016/j.molmet.2018.09.009).

Pivotal trials established efficacy. In STEP 1, once-weekly semaglutide 2.4 mg produced a mean −14.9% body-weight change versus −2.4% with placebo (wilding2021, 10.1056/nejmoa2032183), and outperformed daily liraglutide 3.0 mg in STEP 8 (−15.8% vs −6.4%) (rubino2022, 10.1001/jama.2021.23619). SURMOUNT-1 showed dose-dependent tirzepatide loss up to −20.9% with improvements in all prespecified cardiometabolic measures (jastreboff2022, 10.1056/nejmoa2206038). The head-to-head SURMOUNT-5 trial resolved cross-trial comparison, demonstrating tirzepatide superiority over semaglutide (−20.2% vs −13.7%, P<0.001) (aronne2025, 10.1056/nejmoa2416394).

Cardiovascular and cardiometabolic benefits followed weight loss. In SELECT, semaglutide reduced 3-point MACE (HR 0.80) in patients with established CVD without diabetes (lincoff2023, 10.1056/nejmoa2307563); benefits also extended to heart failure with preserved ejection fraction (kosiborod2023, 10.1056/nejmoa2306963) and, in FLOW, to chronic kidney disease (HR 0.76) (perkovic2024, 10.1056/nejmoa2403347).

Adverse effects are predominantly gastrointestinal—nausea, diarrhea, and vomiting (wharton2021, 10.1111/dom.14551)—and in SELECT adverse-event-driven discontinuation was roughly double placebo (16.6% vs 8.2%) (lincoff2023). Concerns over muscle/lean-mass loss remain unresolved (linge2024, 10.1161/circulationaha.124.067676). Crucially, benefits are not durable: STEP 1 extension showed two-thirds of lost weight regained after withdrawal (net −5.6%) (wilding2022, 10.1111/dom.14725).

Emerging agents target durability, route, and potency. The triple GIP/GLP-1/glucagon agonist retatrutide achieved ≥15% loss in up to 83% of patients (jastreboff2023, 10.1056/nejmoa2301972), while oral options—orforglipron (wharton2023, 10.1056/nejmoa2302392; rosenstock2026, 10.1016/s0140-6736(26)00202-3) and high-dose oral semaglutide (knop2023, 10.1016/s0140-6736(23)01185-6)—offer needle-free alternatives, though cost-effectiveness remains a barrier at current prices (hwang2025, 10.1001/jamahealthforum.2024.5586).
