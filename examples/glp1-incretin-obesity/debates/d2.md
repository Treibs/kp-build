Among oral GLP-1 options for cardiometabolic disease, does a small-molecule nonpeptide (orforglipron) or an oral peptide (oral semaglutide) offer the better benefit-risk profile?

### Orforglipron offers greater glycemic efficacy.
In the ACHIEVE-3 head-to-head trial in type 2 diabetes, orforglipron 12 mg and 36 mg were non-inferior and superior to oral semaglutide 7 mg and 14 mg for HbA1c reduction at 52 weeks (treatment difference -0.48% for 12 mg vs 7 mg).

*Held by:* [[papers/rosenstock2026]], [[papers/wharton2023]]

### The greater efficacy of orforglipron comes with a tolerability trade-off, and oral semaglutide already shows meaningful weight efficacy.
ACHIEVE-3 identified higher rates of gastrointestinal adverse events with orforglipron than oral semaglutide, while high-dose oral semaglutide 50 mg produced superior, clinically meaningful weight loss vs placebo in OASIS 1 — leaving the optimal oral choice unresolved across glycemic vs weight vs tolerability endpoints.

*Held by:* [[papers/rosenstock2026]], [[papers/knop2023]]
