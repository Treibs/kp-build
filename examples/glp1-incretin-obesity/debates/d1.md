Does a dual GIP/GLP-1 agonist (tirzepatide) deliver clinically superior weight loss compared with a GLP-1 receptor agonist (semaglutide) in obesity without diabetes?

### Tirzepatide is superior to semaglutide for weight loss.
The head-to-head SURMOUNT-5 trial directly demonstrated greater weight reduction with maximum-tolerated-dose tirzepatide (-20.2%) than semaglutide 2.4 mg (-13.7%) at 72 weeks (P<0.001), and superiority for waist circumference, resolving the prior reliance on cross-trial comparisons. Preclinical work had already shown the dual agonist decreased weight more potently than a GLP-1 agonist in mice.

*Held by:* [[papers/aronne2025]], [[papers/coskun2018]], [[papers/jastreboff2022]]

### Before the direct trial, the comparison was unresolved and rested on indirect evidence.
Prior to SURMOUNT-5 the comparative efficacy and safety of tirzepatide versus semaglutide in adults with obesity without diabetes was explicitly unknown, with single-agent pivotal trials (SURMOUNT-1 ~20.9% at 15 mg; STEP 1 -14.9%) only comparable across separate trial populations.

*Held by:* [[papers/aronne2025]], [[papers/jastreboff2022]], [[papers/wilding2021]]
