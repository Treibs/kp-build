## Key contributions

- Pivotal phase 3 STEP 1 trial: 1961 adults with overweight/obesity without diabetes randomized 2:1 to once-weekly semaglutide 2.4 mg vs placebo plus lifestyle intervention over 68 weeks.
- Established the magnitude of semaglutide-induced weight loss: -14.9% mean change vs -2.4% with placebo (estimated treatment difference -12.4 percentage points).
- Documented high responder rates (5%, 10%, 15% weight-loss thresholds) and a predominantly mild-to-moderate, transient gastrointestinal adverse-event profile.
- Established nausea and diarrhea as the most common adverse events, typically transient and mild-to-moderate and subsiding over time.
