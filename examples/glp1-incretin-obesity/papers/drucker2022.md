## Key contributions

- Synthesizes how GLP-1 receptor agonists augment glucose-dependent insulin release while reducing glucagon secretion and gastric emptying, and inhibit food intake to reduce body weight.
- Dissects the relative contributions of gut-derived versus brain-derived GLP-1 and of vagal afferent pathways versus central GLP1R+ neuronal populations to the anorectic response.
- Distinguishes energy-expenditure pathways (prominent in preclinical models) from reduction of food intake (dominant in animals and humans) as drivers of weight loss.
- Argues the conserved single-GPCR mechanism plus the T2D safety database support long-term GLP1RA use in obesity, potentially rivaling bariatric surgery.
