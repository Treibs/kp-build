## Key contributions

- Foundational discovery-to-proof-of-concept characterization of LY3298176 (tirzepatide), a fatty-acid-modified peptide engineered as a dual GIP and GLP-1 receptor agonist for once-weekly subcutaneous dosing.
- Demonstrates that the compound activates both GIP and GLP-1 receptor signaling in vitro and produces glucose-dependent insulin secretion and improved glucose tolerance in mice via both receptors.
- Shows that chronic administration in mice decreases body weight and food intake more potently than a selective GLP-1 receptor agonist, motivating the dual-agonist mechanism rationale for adding GIP action to GLP-1.
- Translates preclinical pharmacology to a Phase 1 program showing reductions in fasting glucose and body weight in healthy subjects and patients with T2DM.
