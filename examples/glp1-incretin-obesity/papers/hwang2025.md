## Key contributions

- Lifetime microsimulation cost-effectiveness analysis of tirzepatide, semaglutide, and other antiobesity medications vs lifestyle modification in US adults.
- Finds tirzepatide and semaglutide are not cost-effective at current net prices (ICERs $197,023 and $467,676 per QALY), directly quantifying the access/cost limitation.
- Estimates the price discounts required (30.5% tirzepatide, 81.9% semaglutide) to reach a $100,000/QALY threshold and argues price reductions are essential for equitable access.
