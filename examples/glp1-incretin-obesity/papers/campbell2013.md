## Key contributions

- Establishes the foundational framework that the incretin peptides GLP-1 and GIP regulate islet hormone secretion, glucose concentrations, lipid metabolism, gut motility, appetite, and body weight, providing the scientific basis for incretin-based therapies in type 2 diabetes.
- Distinguishes direct actions of incretin receptors on tissues expressing them from indirect mechanisms mediated through neuronal and endocrine pathways.
- Contrasts the pharmacology versus physiology of incretin hormones and reviews how incretin receptor signaling couples to pleiotropic metabolic actions.
