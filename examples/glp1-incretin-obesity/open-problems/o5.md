Comparative long-term efficacy and safety across the rapidly expanding class — dual vs triple agonists, injectable vs oral, peptide vs small-molecule — is largely established only on short-to-medium-term surrogate endpoints (weight, HbA1c) rather than head-to-head hard-outcome data.

**Why it matters:** Clinicians and payers must choose among many agents; without head-to-head outcome trials, sequencing and selection rest on indirect comparison and surrogate endpoints.
