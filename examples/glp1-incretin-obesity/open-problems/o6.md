The relative mechanistic contributions to weight loss — central vs peripheral GLP-1 action, vagal vs central GLP1R+ neuronal pathways, and reduced food intake vs altered energy expenditure — are not fully resolved, and the added value of GIP and glucagon co-agonism over GLP-1 alone is still being mapped to clinical effect.

**Why it matters:** Understanding which mechanisms drive efficacy guides rational design of next-generation single, dual, and triple agonists and predicts which combinations will translate to durable clinical benefit.
