Why it matters: The aggregation rule directly determines the gradient the policy receives; a poor aggregation wastes informative criteria, uniformly supervises correct and incorrect steps, and opens the door to reward hacking via self-correction.

Rubric-based RL is a promising route for extending reinforcement learning beyond verifiable rewards, yet existing methods optimize rubrics while treating the query distribution as fixed. We identify a structural bottleneck: rubric quality is constrained by query structure.
