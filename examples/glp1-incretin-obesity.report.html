Does a dual GIP/GLP-1 agonist (tirzepatide) deliver clinically superior weight loss compared with a GLP-1 receptor agonist (semaglutide) in obesity without diabetes? resolved
Tirzepatide is superior to semaglutide for weight loss. aronne2025 coskun2018 jastreboff2022
The head-to-head SURMOUNT-5 trial directly demonstrated greater weight reduction with maximum-tolerated-dose tirzepatide (-20.2%) than semaglutide 2.4 mg (-13.7%) at 72 weeks (P<0.001), and superiority for waist circumference, resolving the prior reliance on cross-trial comparisons. Preclinical work had already shown the dual agonist decreased weight more potently than a GLP-1 agonist in mice.
Before the direct trial, the comparison was unresolved and rested on indirect evidence. aronne2025 jastreboff2022 wilding2021
Prior to SURMOUNT-5 the comparative efficacy and safety of tirzepatide versus semaglutide in adults with obesity without diabetes was explicitly unknown, with single-agent pivotal trials (SURMOUNT-1 ~20.9% at 15 mg; STEP 1 -14.9%) only comparable across separate trial populations.
Among oral GLP-1 options for cardiometabolic disease, does a small-molecule nonpeptide (orforglipron) or an oral peptide (oral semaglutide) offer the better benefit-risk profile?
Orforglipron offers greater glycemic efficacy. rosenstock2026 wharton2023
In the ACHIEVE-3 head-to-head trial in type 2 diabetes, orforglipron 12 mg and 36 mg were non-inferior and superior to oral semaglutide 7 mg and 14 mg for HbA1c reduction at 52 weeks (treatment difference -0.48% for 12 mg vs 7 mg).
The greater efficacy of orforglipron comes with a tolerability trade-off, and oral semaglutide already shows meaningful weight efficacy. rosenstock2026 knop2023
ACHIEVE-3 identified higher rates of gastrointestinal adverse events with orforglipron than oral semaglutide, while high-dose oral semaglutide 50 mg produced superior, clinically meaningful weight loss vs placebo in OASIS 1 — leaving the optimal oral choice unresolved across glycemic vs weight vs tolerability endpoints.
