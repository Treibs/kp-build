Do SWE-bench leaderboard scores measure genuine engineering ability or training-data memorization?

### Scores are inflated by contamination/memorization and poor generalization.
Models do ~3x better at resolving and ~6x better at localizing edited files on Verified than on decontaminated sets; Python-tuned systems fail to generalize to JavaScript/visual tasks; harder contamination-resistant benchmarks recalibrate scores downward.

*Held by:* [[papers/prathifkumar2025swememory]], [[papers/yang2024swebenchmm]], [[papers/deng2025swebenchpro]]

### The benchmark measures a real, hard capability that is genuinely improving.
SWE-bench was built from real issue→PR pairs with real test validation and started at <2% resolve; subsequent training/RL advances (SWE-RL 41%, SWE-agent-LM 40.2%) show real, test-validated gains rather than pure recall.

*Held by:* [[papers/jimenez2024swebench]], [[papers/wei2025swerl]], [[papers/yang2025swesmith]]
