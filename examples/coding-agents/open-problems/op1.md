Benchmark contamination / memorization confounds SWE-bench leaderboard scores: models do markedly better on the public Verified set than on decontaminated sets given identical context, so reported resolve rates may overstate genuine agent ability.

**Why it matters:** If headline numbers reflect training-data recall rather than reasoning, the entire 'progress' narrative on SWE-bench is unreliable; contamination-resistant benchmarks (held-out/commercial splits) are the partial fix but not yet standard practice.
