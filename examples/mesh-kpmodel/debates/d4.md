Monofilament vs multifilament yarn for mesh: which best balances strength, water/weight, abrasion, and stiffness/hold?

### Monofilament minimizes water-trapping surface and is the basis of the best-characterized knot/strength behavior, and larger-diameter mono gives higher breaking load.
In PA6 nets, higher yarn diameter gives higher breaking load and elongation, monofilament knot behavior is the reference case for knot-strength loss, and nylon monofilament has the best knot-fatigue durability, so a monofilament construction offers predictable strength and less internal water-trapping surface area.

*Held by:* [[papers/Thomas2006SunlightPA6]], [[papers/LeGue2024KnotBiodegradable]], [[papers/Wanchana2002HPPEFatigue]]

### Multifilament yarns and high-performance polyethylene filament give superior tensile-fatigue and abrasion behavior, relevant to long service life.
HPPE/UHMWPE filament has the highest tensile-fatigue durability and least abrasion damage and an exceptional strength-to-weight ratio; the PA6 sunlight study covers both mono- and multifilament, indicating multifilament/filament constructions can win on fatigue and abrasion where mono wins on knot behavior, so the choice trades knot-strength predictability against fatigue/abrasion life.

*Held by:* [[papers/Wanchana2002HPPEFatigue]], [[papers/faruk2023uhmwpe]], [[papers/Thomas2006SunlightPA6]]
