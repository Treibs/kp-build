Water saturation drops the glass transition temperature of polyamide 6.6 from 58 C (dry) to about -24 C (saturated), a reduction exceeding 80 K, consistent with a water-induced plasticizing effect.

> The study documented a dramatic Tg drop: from 58 C (dry) to -24 C (water-saturated) - a reduction exceeding 80 K. The researchers noted this shift aligns with water-induced plasticizing effect.

— [[papers/Wetzel2023]] (doi:10.3390/polym15163387)
