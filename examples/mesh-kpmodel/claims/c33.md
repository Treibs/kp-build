Hindered amine light stabilizers (HALS) protect polymers without absorbing UV, instead cycling through nitroxyl radicals that scavenge alkyl and peroxy radicals to interrupt photo-oxidation in polyolefins, polyamides, and rubbers.

> The tetramethylpiperidine moiety, which is the basic structure of HALS, is initially oxidized to produce a nitroxyl radical by UV irradiation. This nitroxyl radical then acts as a scavenger of an alkyl radical originating from the substrate polymer chain to form an alkylated aminoether. From the aminoether, the nitroxyl radical is regenerated in turn through quenching another peroxyradical.

— [[papers/ChirinosPadron1990HALS]] (doi:10.1080/07366579008050906)
