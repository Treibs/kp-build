Absorbed water plasticizes polyamide 6 by increasing macromolecular chain mobility, which causes a large decrease in both Young's modulus and yield stress while increasing elongation at break.

> The presence of water in PA6 leads to a large modification of mechanical properties induced by an increase in the mobility of macromolecular chains. This increase in mobility leads to a decrease in both Young's modulus and yield stress, whereas elongation at break is increased.

— [[papers/LeGac2017]] (doi:10.1016/j.polymdegradstab.2017.02.003)
