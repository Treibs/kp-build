## Key contributions

- Directly addresses lacrosse mesh material/coating trade-offs: argues a hydrophobic polypropylene yarn (INNEGRA S, density 0.84 g/cm3) is intrinsically water-resistant and needs no wax/polymer coating, unlike nylon/cotton
- Quantifies that conventional nylon (1.15 g/cm3) and cotton (1.54 g/cm3) absorb liquid and gain weight, changing pocket consistency and flexibility when wet
- Claims the uncoated hydrophobic mesh is about 50% lighter than conventional hard, wax, or soft mesh and stretches no more than ~11%; notes prior-art coatings deteriorate or require replenishment over time
