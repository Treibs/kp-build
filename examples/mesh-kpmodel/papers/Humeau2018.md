## Key contributions

- Directly studies nylon 6 fibre yarns: moisture reduces the Tg of the amorphous structure (Tg ~55 C dry at 0%RH), small effect on short-term tensile behaviour but strong effect on long-term creep
- Identifies a Schapery non-linear viscoelastic/viscoplastic creep model; instantaneous compliance increases with stress and RH, and recovery shows permanent (irreversible) elongation
- Concludes moisture-driven creep must be quantified for any long-term immersed nylon application, the mechanistic basis for wet elongation / bagging out
