Judge-model bias and self-preference contaminate rubric rewards: self-preference bias persists even with objective verifiable rubrics, and ensembling judges mitigates but does not eliminate it.

**Why it matters:** In recursive self-improvement loops where the policy and judge share a family or backbone, self-preference bias systematically inflates the policy's own outputs, corrupting the training signal in ways simple objectivity checks do not catch.
