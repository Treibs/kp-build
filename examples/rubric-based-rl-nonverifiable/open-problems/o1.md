Aggregating multi-criterion rubric judgments into a single scalar reward is not principled: static aggregations conflate a criterion's human-assigned importance with its current usefulness as a training signal, lose multi-criterion structure, and can mis-reward individual reasoning steps.

**Why it matters:** The aggregation rule directly determines the gradient the policy receives; a poor aggregation wastes informative criteria, uniformly supervises correct and incorrect steps, and opens the door to reward hacking via self-correction.
