There is no standardized non-verifiable evaluation protocol for validating that a rubric reward correlates with true human-judged quality, so methods are validated against heterogeneous benchmarks, judge panels, and self-defined diagnostics.

**Why it matters:** Without a shared protocol for confirming rubric-reward fidelity, reported gains across papers are hard to compare and may reflect benchmark-specific or judge-specific artifacts rather than real quality improvements.
