Should the rubric reward be a single pointwise scalar per response, or should criterion-level signals be kept disaggregated (step-wise, pairwise, or externally aggregated)?

### Pointwise scalar aggregation is the workable baseline and can be made effective by improving the rubric and adaptive weighting.
The original RaR formulation scores rollouts against structured criteria and aggregates them into a single scalar reward that drives on-policy RL, beating Likert LLM-as-judge baselines; later work keeps the pointwise paradigm but improves it via policy-aware criterion weighting and better rubric generation.

*Held by:* [[papers/gunjal2025]], [[papers/tyagi2026]], [[papers/shen2026]]

### Single-scalar aggregation is fundamentally lossy and should be replaced by disaggregated step-wise or pairwise criterion signals.
Collapsing rubric scores into one response-level scalar loses multi-criterion structure, uniformly supervises correct and incorrect steps, and enables reward hacking; step-wise attribution, pairwise criterion comparison aggregated externally, and probability-based scoring are proposed as remedies that avoid pointwise weighted scalarization.

*Held by:* [[papers/xie2026]], [[papers/jia2026]], [[papers/jiang2026]]
