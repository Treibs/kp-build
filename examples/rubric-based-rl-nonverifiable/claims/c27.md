Training a reward model for chain-of-thought demands heavy human labeling effort; RLCER instead rewards CoTs with self-proposed and self-evolving rubrics that require no human annotation and provide reliable supervision even without outcome rewards.

> training a reward model demands heavy human labeling efforts, and static RMs struggle with evolving CoT distributions and reward hacking. These challenges motivate us to seek an autonomous CoT rewarding approach that requires no human annotation efforts and can evolve gradually. Inspired by recent self-evolving training methods, we propose RLCER (Reinforcement Learning with CoT Supervision via Self-Evolving Rubrics), which enhances the outcome-centric RLVR by rewarding CoTs with self-proposed and self-evolving rubrics. We show that self-proposed and self-evolving rubrics provide reliable CoT supervision signals even without outcome rewards
