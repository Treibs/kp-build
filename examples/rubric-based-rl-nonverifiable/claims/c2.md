RaR is motivated by the fact that real-world reasoning evaluation depends on nuanced, multi-criteria judgments rather than binary correctness, and uses instance-specific rubrics as reward signals for on-policy post-training.

> However, extending it to real-world reasoning tasks is challenging, as evaluation depends on nuanced, multi-criteria judgments rather than binary correctness. Instance-specific rubrics have recently been used in evaluation benchmarks to capture such judgments, but their potential as reward signals for on-policy post-training remains underexplored.

— [[papers/gunjal2025]] (arXiv:2507.17746)
