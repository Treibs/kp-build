AMARIS stores rollout analyses, step-level summaries, and rubric update records in a persistent evaluation memory and retrieves recent, semantically relevant history to revise rubrics, running asynchronously alongside the RL loop to reduce blocking latency relative to synchronous rubric updates.

> AMARIS stores rollout analyses, step-level summaries, and rubric update records in a persistent evaluation memory, then retrieves recent and semantically relevant history to revise rubrics. We evaluate AMARIS across science, medicine, instruction following, and creative writing under both global and instance-specific rubric settings. AMARIS improves over static, local-adaptive, and memory-ablated baselines, such as +2.8 points on GPQA-Diamond and +2.2 points on IFBench over the strongest baselines, while analysis shows that memory reduces oscillatory rubric edits and supports a progression from early failure correction to later curriculum advancement. AMARIS runs asynchronously alongside the normal RL loop, reducing blocking latency relative to synchronous rubric updates.
