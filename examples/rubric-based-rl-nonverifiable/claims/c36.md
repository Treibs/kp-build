Most prior work mitigates reward hacking by limiting the policy update with a KL penalty toward a reference model, while gradient regularization instead biases training toward flatter regions that maintain reward-model accuracy.

> A common problem is reward hacking, where the policy may exploit inaccuracies of the reward and learn an unintended behavior. Most previous works address this by limiting the policy update with a Kullback-Leibler (KL) penalty towards a reference model. We propose a different framing: Train the LM in a way that biases policy updates towards regions in which the reward is more accurate.

— [[papers/ackermann2026]] (arXiv:2602.18037)
