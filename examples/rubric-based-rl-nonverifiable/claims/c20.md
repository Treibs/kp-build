In rubric-based RL, where an LLM-as-a-Judge scores model outputs according to rubrics as rewards, policy models may exploit latent biases in the judge, leading to reward hacking and ineffective or unsafe training outcomes.

> Rubric-based reinforcement learning (RL) uses an LLM-as-a-Judge (LaaJ) to score model outputs according to rubrics as rewards. However, policy models may exploit latent biases in the judge, leading to reward hacking and ineffective or unsafe training outcomes.
