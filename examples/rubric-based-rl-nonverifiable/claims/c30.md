Existing adaptive rubric methods update criteria from local evidence such as the current batch or instance-level comparisons, which discards diagnostic information and makes it hard to track recurring failures, evaluate previous rubric edits, or raise standards once earlier criteria become saturated.

> existing adaptive rubric methods typically update criteria from local evidence such as the current batch or instance-level comparisons. This local view discards diagnostic information produced during training, making it difficult to track recurring failures, evaluate previous rubric edits, or raise standards once earlier criteria become saturated.
