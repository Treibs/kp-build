## Key contributions

- Diagnoses that RaR aggregates rubric scores into a single response-level scalar, losing multi-criterion structure and enabling reward hacking via self-correction (18.2% wrong steps positively rewarded; 49.9% correct steps penalized)
- Proposes SRaR, attributing each rubric item to a specific reasoning step via an LLM judge, normalizing per-step scores across rollouts, and combining with outcome reward via a decoupled advantage estimator
- Improves over RaR by 3.57 points (Qwen3-8B) and 2.75 points (Qwen3-32B) on six math benchmarks, raising Faithful Reasoning Rate on AIME 2025 from 34.5% to 46.7% and cutting self-correction looping from 48.1% to 26.5%
