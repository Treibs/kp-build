## Key contributions

- Studies reward hacking in rubric-based RL by optimizing against a training verifier while evaluating against a cross-family panel of three frontier judges
- Separates verifier failure from rubric-design limitations and catalogs recurring exploitation failure modes (partial satisfaction of compound criteria, treating implicit as explicit, imprecise topical matching)
- Introduces the self-internalization gap, a verifier-free diagnostic based on policy log-probabilities, and shows stronger verification reduces but does not eliminate reward hacking, with rubric gains not necessarily corresponding to broader quality gains
