## Key contributions

- Introduces CHERRL, a controllable hacking environment for rubric-based RL that injects known biases into the LLM-as-a-Judge to reproducibly study reward hacking
- Enables stable reproduction of reward divergence and precise identification of hacking onset as a clean testbed for mitigations
- Analyzes judge biases by discoverability and exploitability and explores an agent-based system to automatically detect reward-hacking onset from training logs
