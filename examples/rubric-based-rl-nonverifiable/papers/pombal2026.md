## Key contributions

- First study of self-preference bias (SPB) in rubric-based binary-criterion evaluation, relevant to recursive self-improvement training loops
- Shows SPB persists even with objective verifiable rubrics (judges up to 50% more likely to mark own failed outputs as satisfied)
- Finds ensembling judges mitigates but does not eliminate SPB, and identifies negative rubrics, extreme lengths, and subjective topics as risk factors
