The problem

Confident, but wrong — and everyone pays for it twice

Large language models sound authoritative on recent, specialized research. In reality they hallucinate papers that don't exist, miss anything past their training cut-off, and average competing results into something bland and wrong.

So every team's AI re-spends compute reconstructing the same research landscape — and often grounds real work (papers, strategy, product decisions) in sources that were never checked, or never existed. That is wasted money and quiet reputational risk, repeated across every project.

What it is

A fact-checked briefing your AI can load

A knowledge package: a portable, verified, AI-loadable map of a research topic — the key papers (all confirmed real), the open problems, the live debates, the headline results.

Build it once

An automated research pass produces the package and verifies every citation. It becomes a reusable asset — not a throwaway report.

Every agent inherits it

Any AI loads the package and starts from a verified, current landscape — no re-research, no invented sources, no stale picture.

How it works

Five steps — and verification is non-negotiable

Research

AI agents survey the literature, then follow the citation graph — what each key paper cites and is cited by — to catch the seminal and the newest work a keyword search misses.

Verify the load-bearing guarantee

Every citation is checked against real databases (arXiv, Crossref). A fabricated or mislabeled source is dropped, not shipped. This is what makes the package trustworthy.

Package

The verified knowledge is structured into a portable, AI-loadable format — spine of papers, open problems, debates, and reported results.

Measure does it actually help?

A built-in test pits an AI with the package against an unaided AI on a held-out task, and scores the difference. No hand-waving — a number.

Share

The package ships in an open, standard format, so anyone can install and reuse it like a software dependency. Build once; the whole team — or ecosystem — benefits.

The proof

Tested on a topic the AI is genuinely weak at

We ran it on diffusion language models — a fast-moving field where roughly half the important work is newer than the AI's training. Every core citation verified live, and the package measurably improved the AI's research grounding:

Unaided, the AI could ground only a fraction of the field and mislabeled 3 of its citations (right ID, wrong paper). Loaded with the package, it cited the real, verified landscape — no errors. Following the citation graph also surfaced 560+ real related papers the first keyword survey had missed.

Honest by design: confidence is always stated relative to the sources, every citation is checked rather than asserted, and the package is a research foundation — a verified starting point — not a finished paper.
